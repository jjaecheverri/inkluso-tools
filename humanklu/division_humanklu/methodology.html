HKP v1.1 — Methodology

How HumanKlu certifies AI-generated intelligence

1. What Gets Certified

We certify AI-generated intelligence reports. Each report contains claims. Each claim is labeled VERIFIED (has a source URL that can be independently checked) or INFERRED (no source URL — the claim is derived from reasoning, synthesis, or general knowledge).

The ratio of inferred claims to total claims is the inferred_ratio. A report where 3 out of 5 claims are inferred has an inferred_ratio of 0.60. This ratio directly affects certification eligibility.

2. Five Scoring Dimensions

Every report is scored across five dimensions, each on a 0–10 scale:

Dimension Code What It Measures
Evidence Integrity EVID Are claims supported by traceable, credible sources?
Mechanism Clarity MECH Does the report explain how and why things happen, not just what?
Incentive Decode INC Does the report identify who benefits, who pays, and what motivates the actors?
Risk Realism RISK Are risks presented with proportion and probability, not just worst-case scenarios?
Specificity SPEC Does the report contain concrete numbers, dates, names, and falsifiable predictions?

3. EVID Ceiling Rule

If a report has too many inferred claims, the Evidence score gets capped to reflect the reduced verifiability:

Inferred Ratio EVID Cap
> 75%max 6.5
> 60%max 7.2
≤ 60%no cap

We store both evid_raw (the original score) and evid_effective (the score after ceiling is applied). The effective score is what feeds into HCI calculation and certification thresholds.

4. Absence Assertion Detection

If a claim contains phrases like "no third-party audit", "no audit", "no evidence found", or "no public audit" AND has no source URL, it is flagged as ABSENCE_ASSERTION.

An absence assertion is a claim that something does not exist — but without a source to prove the absence. These flags are significant: they block certification above HK-REVIEWED, because asserting the non-existence of evidence without sourcing that assertion is itself an evidence integrity failure.

5. HCI Calculation

The Human Calibration Index (HCI) is the simple average of the five effective dimension scores:

HCI = average(EVID_effective, MECH, INC, RISK, SPEC)

This single number summarizes overall report quality, but it is never the sole determinant of certification level. Individual dimension thresholds and flags also apply.

6. Certification Thresholds

Each certification level requires meeting all of the following constraints:

Level HCI ≥ EVID_eff ≥ Inferred Ratio ≤ SPEC ≥ Extra Constraints
HK-REJECTED — — — — Assigned when any higher level's minimum is not met
HK-REVIEWED 5.5 5.0 1.0 — None
HK-VERIFIED 7.0 7.0 0.40 6.5 No ABSENCE_ASSERTION flags
HK-PRO 7.8 7.5 0.25 7.0 No ABSENCE_ASSERTION flags; second-pass review required
HK-INSTITUTIONAL 8.2 8.0 0.15 7.5 No ABSENCE_ASSERTION flags; two independent reviews required

7. Why We Sometimes Downgrade

A report with excellent scores can still be downgraded if too many claims lack sources or if absence assertions are present. This is by design. Reputation-grade certification must reflect actual evidence availability, not just analytical quality. A brilliantly written report that relies heavily on inferred claims cannot receive the same trust mark as one backed by verifiable sources — no matter how insightful the analysis.
